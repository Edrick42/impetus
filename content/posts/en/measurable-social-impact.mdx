"Social impact" has become a panel term. Every nonprofit talks about it. But what separates a nice speech from real work?

## Three signs an organization takes impact seriously

1. **It has clear metrics** — and shares them publicly, even when the numbers are uncomfortable
2. **It tells specific stories** — with names, dates and contexts, not just generic sentences
3. **It accepts being audited** — by press, by academia, by whoever wants to look

## What we track

At Instituto Impetus, we're still building our measurement framework. Some questions guiding the process:

- How many entrepreneurs gained access to investment after participating in our initiatives?
- How many contracts were closed as a direct result of the meetings we facilitated?
- What percentage of supported businesses remain active 12 months later?

## Why this matters

Because without numbers, anyone can claim to be changing the world. With numbers, you commit to improving — or to explaining why you didn't.
