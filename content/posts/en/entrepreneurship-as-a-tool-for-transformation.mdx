The favela is not a problem — it is potential. That's the first lesson we learn when we look closely at the entrepreneurial ecosystem of Brazilian communities.

## What we're seeing in practice

In recent years, events like Favela + Rica have proven something that isolated statistics had already hinted at: there is a robust, creative and resilient economy inside the favelas. What's missing — almost never talent. It's capital, mentorship, access to networks that have historically been closed.

> "Talent exists everywhere. What's missing is opportunity."

When a favela entrepreneur gains access to an investor, an experienced mentor, or simply a stage to present their business, the impact multiplies. This is not charity. It is recognition that the Brazilian entrepreneurial ecosystem is incomplete without these voices.

## The role of Instituto Impetus

Our role as official sponsor of Favela + Rica 2026 is not symbolic. It is a strategic position: we want to be where these connections happen, helping open doors that should have been open for a long time.

There's work that happens before the event and work that happens after. The two-day gathering is just the visible part.
